TV Series

By Francisco Alexandre

The Leftovers

Three years after the disappearance of 2% of the global human population, a group of people in a small New York community try to continue their lives while coping with the tragedy of the unexplained nature of the event.

One of the best series I've ever seen in my life, I watched it without expectations and finished with a sense of accomplishment for watching this work of art. The fact that it focuses on people is the highlight of the series.

The Sopranos

New Jersey mob boss Tony Soprano deals with personal and professional issues in his home and business life that affect his mental state, leading him to seek professional psychiatric counseling.

I think movies that show mafias of Italian origin are cool, and the series delves quite deeply into how the Sopranos family operates. One of the things I liked most about the series was how thoroughly it explored the characters.

House

Using a crack team of doctors and his wits, an antisocial maverick doctor specializing in diagnostic medicine does whatever it takes to solve puzzling cases that come his way.

I've watched it several times throughout my life; I consider it an addictive series that's impossible to get tired of. The characters are captivating throughout the plot.

Breaking Bad

A chemistry teacher diagnosed with inoperable lung cancer turns to manufacturing and selling methamphetamine with a former student to secure his family's future.

One of the best series of all time, an audiovisual work of art, I always recommend it to everyone.

Better Call Saul

The trials and tribulations of criminal lawyer Jimmy McGill in the years leading up to his fateful run-in with Walter White and Jesse Pinkman.

Just like Breaking Bad, it's perfect. It's an expansion on the Breaking Bad story, I always recommend everyone watch it.
